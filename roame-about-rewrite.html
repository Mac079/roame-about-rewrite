The Beginning

Melbourne elegance.
Los Angeles ease.
A world in between.

Hollie Sweet spent over 15 years in the fashion industry, including running one of Australia's most respected premium fashion agencies. She understood how clothes were made, how they were sold, and what was consistently missing: resort wear with genuine creative ambition behind it.

ROAME was the answer to that gap. A Melbourne-based label designed for women who move between places and want their wardrobe to move with them. Each collection is a chapter. Each print is a specific place, rendered in cloth so you can wear it long after the trip is over.

The name says everything. Not tourist, not traveller. Something freer than both. Someone who goes where the feeling takes them and dresses accordingly.

The ROAME Woman

She dresses like
her holiday self
more often.

She has a passport with real stamps in it. She knows the difference between tourist and traveller and she prefers neither word. She collects places the way other people collect things, and she wants her wardrobe to reflect that.

She is not dressing for an occasion. She is dressing for the kind of life where occasion and everyday blur together. For the dinner that starts on the terrace and moves somewhere unexpected. For the trip that turns into something more. ROAME was made for her and for the particular ease that comes from wearing something that already knows how to travel.

Copywriter's Notes
What changed and why

The Hero

The live ROAME About page opens with: "Ever-cool, passionate, and free-spirited, ROAME. is an Australian ready-to-wear label that transports the individual to wistful travel memories and new horizons." That is a category description written as a brand statement. Hollie's own words to a stockist are far more specific and far more evocative: "our bespoke prints are a creative journey capturing a moment in time." The rewrite leads with that line because it is the most honest and specific thing said publicly about what ROAME actually does. Specificity converts. Wanderlust clichés do not.

The Print Philosophy Section

The Scarf Print is a tribute to St Germain and the Cafe de Flore. The Vacances Print takes you to Plage du Petit Sperone in Corsica. These are not vague resort vibes. They are specific places with specific feelings attached to them. The current About page copy is entirely generic about the prints. The rewrite gives each print its own moment because that specificity is the brand's strongest asset. Anyone can say "tells tales of distant shores." Almost no one can say "the rattan texture of the classic French green bistro chair at four in the afternoon."

The Hollie Quote

Hollie Sweet does not appear anywhere on the current ROAME website. There is no founder presence, no personal voice, no sense of who built this brand or why. Yet Hollie has 15 years of industry experience through The LOFFT Fashion Agency and a clear, specific creative sensibility. The rewrite surfaces her directly as the voice behind the brand. At this price point and positioning, founder presence is a conversion asset. Customers want to know who made the decisions. The current site does not tell them.

The Newsletter

"JOIN OUR NEWSLETTER FOR 15% OFF YOUR FIRST ORDER" is the current newsletter offer. For a brand whose prints are specific tributes to Paris cafes and Corsican beaches, a 15% discount as the newsletter hook is a significant mismatch. The rewrite positions the newsletter as access to the stories behind each print and early access to new arrivals. "Written for the women who want to know where the clothes came from before they decide where to wear them" speaks directly to the ROAME customer. She is buying a story as much as a dress. The newsletter should tell it.

Before and After: The About Opening
Live copy

"Ever-cool, passionate, and free-spirited, ROAME. is an Australian ready-to-wear label that transports the individual to wistful travel memories and new horizons. Let the eternal vacation begin."

Category language dressed up as brand voice. "Wistful travel memories" and "eternal vacation" are phrases that appear on hundreds of resort wear sites. The copy could belong to anyone.
Rewrite

"Every ROAME collection begins the same way. A place. A memory. A particular quality of light at a specific hour in a city Hollie has loved. The clothes are made to carry that feeling, so the woman wearing them carries it too."

Grounds the brand in a specific creative process. Names the founder. Makes the purpose of the clothes felt rather than stated. Could only be ROAME.
